Chapter 01

The Italian fisherman had not seen the Frenchman for a long time. It was about a month ago on the coast of Marseille and he was at the fish market. So he took four cards from the pile and arranged them in a row, one below the other. The Frenchman looked at them attentively to try and guess their meaning.

The first card was the Moon. The Frenchman interpreted it as a time reference because he remembered seeing the Italian fisherman about a month earlier.

The second card was the Magician, which depicted a man standing at a table handling something. This card confirmed his first intuition and he associated the man on the card with the man he had met in Marseilles.

The third card was the two of coins, which in this sequence of cards clearly symbolised a trade between him and the other man.

The last card was the World, but it was presented as a question because the Italian man helped himself by spinning his finger over the card as if to say: ‘Where on earth did you disappear to?’ ‘What on earth has happened to you since then?
