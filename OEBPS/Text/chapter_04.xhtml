Chapter 04

The Frenchman recalled another episode related to money, but it was a sad story. Nothing about him betrayed the anger and shame that his next story aroused in him. No blinking, no trembling lips, no shrugging of shoulders. He remained impassive like the statue of a sage, and only the cards would describe, however cryptically, his trouble. So, he took the pile of cards and started selecting the cards for his story.

He too started his short story with the card of the Tower as it was the best to describe his misfortune. The man falling from the tower could not but presage damage, an accident, a loss, or at the very least a change. The Italian fisher could not guess it from the looks of his French counterpart.

The Chariot card also had an unequivocal meaning. It could symbolise a journey by land on horseback or by carriage, following the accident described in the Tower card. Or perhaps he had to move to another place because he had lost his home.

The aim of this trip became clear on his third move, when he played the Judge card. The Frenchman had to pay legal fees. This could be a fine, a tax or a debt.

The cards could not describe more accurately the type of misfortune and the type of payment that the Frenchman had to endure. The 10 of Pentacles card showed, however, that it was a bitter experience, as it was clear that he had lost a lot of money because of this event.
