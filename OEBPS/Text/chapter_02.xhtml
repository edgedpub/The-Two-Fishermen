Chapter 02

The Frenchman started to enjoy the game, but was overwhelmed by both positive and negative feelings. He appreciated his colleague's attention and was happy that he had noticed his absence. Once he understood the rules of the game, he began to think of a way to respond in the same way.

This reminded him of the vicissitudes of the past three months, which had caused him pain. Of all these feelings, not one leaked from his facial expression and all these thoughts, feelings of happiness and sorrow translated into a nod of his head. Meanwhile, he finished eating his buridda and imagined how to summarise a long and intricate story with cards.

The first card he pulled was The Lover to represent the young couple. He pointed the finger to the girl on the right side of the image and then to himself to signal that she was his daughter. He was overjoyed about the wedding, but as with the other emotions, no part of his body gave away any emotion.

The second card was the 9 of Pentacles. Money was the first association the Italian man made and his first interpretation was confirmed by a hint from the Frenchman, who rubbed his thumb on the index finger - a hand gesture common to both of them.

The last card of the triplet was the Hermite. The Italian pondered the meaning of this card for a while. Eventually, he concluded that the wandering man on the card symbolised his French friend and was the answer to his question. In this story, it could only mean that the Frenchman had wandered off to look for money.
